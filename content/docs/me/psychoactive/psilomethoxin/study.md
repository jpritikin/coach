# Informed Consent

**Study Title**: Double-blind randomized cross-over study of Psilocybe with and without 5-MeO-DMT enrichment: feels different?

{{% hint info %}}
This is how you agree to join the study. If you are curious, please read this consent agreement carefully before you decide to participate.
{{% /hint %}}

**Introduction**: In September 2021, Ian Benouis tried growing a new type of psychoactive mushroom. He gave it to some religious people who use psychedelics, and they judged it to be an important new drug. They called it psilomethoxin, but some people didn't believe it was different from regular magic mushrooms (psilocybin/psilocin).

**Purpose of the research study**: This study will investigate whether people can tell the difference between psilomethoxin and regular magic mushrooms in a blinded condition.

**What you will do in the study**:

- You will complete a demographic questionnaire.
- You will attend an in-person introductory meeting. The goals of this meeting are to (a) determine a comparable dose of psilocybin for this group and (b) familiarize you with the study procedures. Upon arrival, each participant will ingest one 250mg capsule of psilomethoxin or a roughly equivalent amount of psilocybin. After one hour, you will be asked to write down your best estimate for a comparable dose of psilocybin.
- The next day, you will receive a 31-day pill organizer with the same number of indistinguishable, opaque pills for every day.
- Every day for the next 31 days, swallow the assigned pills in the morning. Please do not chew or otherwise peek inside the pills.
- Every evening for the next 31 days, you will be asked to complete a brief survey.
- After 31 days, you will receive a final survey.
- You can discontinue this study at any time.

**Time required**: The demographic questionnaire will take less than 10 minutes. The in-person introductory meeting will take about 75 minutes. On the following day, you will need to pick up your pill organizer. Daily surveys will take less than 5 minutes. The final survey will take about 10 minutes.

**Risks**:

- The Church of Sacred Synthesis has distributed at least 500,000 doses of psilomethoxin without any report of an adverse event. However, psilomethoxin is a new substance with only 2-3 years of anecdotal safety data. Research is ongoing, but little about the substance is known for certain. Until we learn more, it is prudent to regard contraindications for 5-MeO-DMT as contraindications for psilomethoxin. The contraindications include epilepsy, CYP2D6 inhibitors, MAO inhibitors, serotonin reuptake inhibitors, lithium, pregnancy, or breastfeeding.
- There is a small risk of loss of confidentiality since we require your name, email address, and mobile phone number to communicate survey invitations. To minimize this risk, all information will be stored in password protected computers and transferred via encrypted channels. Within one week after the completion of data collection, identifying information will be destroyed.
- All participants will together attend an in-person introductory meeting. We ask that no photographs be taken, but it is possible that some participants will recognize each other.

**Benefits**:
- This study may help establish that psilomethoxin has different subjective effects compared to regular magic mushrooms. We expect this will spur research and lead to many further benefits.
- You may find the use of psilomethoxin personally rewarding and may enjoy continuing to access this substance.
- Anonymized data will be archived on a freely accessible website, available for other researchers to analyze.

**Costs**: There is no cost for participating in this study other than your time investment.

**Confidentiality**:
- Attendees of the in-person introductory meeting cannot avoid seeing each other. We ask that knowledge of attendance at the event be kept confidential so that only attendees might know who else attended.
- Potentially identifiable information about you consists of your name, email address, and mobile phone number (if provided). These data will be stored in a password protected computer and only transmitted over encrypted channels. Within one week after the completion of data collection, these data will be destroyed, rendering the data de-identified.

**Voluntary participation and withdrawal**: Your participation in this study is voluntary. You may decide to not participate in this study. Your decision not to take part will involve no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.
