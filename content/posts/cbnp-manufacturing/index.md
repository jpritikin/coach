{{< hint warning >}}
Why CBN-P is nearly impossible to buy and how a botched batch is quietly poisoning the market
{{< /hint >}}

CBN-P (cannabiphorol) is the seven-carbon-chain homolog of CBN. The "-phorol" suffix denotes the
same heptyl chain found in THCP. I used it daily for almost six months. It dramatically
improved my emotional regulation in the evenings — details at
[my cannabis sleep page]({{< relref "/docs/me/psychoactive/cannabis/sleep" >}}).

Pleasantly dependent on this marvelous cannabinoid, I ordered 1 kg
from [MC Nutraceuticals](https://mcnutraceuticals.com/) on November
18, 2025 (SO-018519, $8,700). Then the COA arrived.

## The COA

{{< figure src="coa.webp" alt="Certificate of Analysis showing CBNP at 74.6% with 16.93% total HHCP contamination" >}}

CBNP came in at 74.6% (746 mg/g). Fine. CBN at 0.265% — no big deal. But then:

| Analyte | Result (%) | Result (mg/g) |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| 9R-HHCP | 14.6 | 146 |
| 9S-HHCP | 2.33 | 23.3 |
| **Total HHCP** | **16.93** | **169.3** |

Total cannabinoids: 91.9%. So HHCP represents 16.93 ÷ 91.9 = **18.4% of active material**.

At a modest 5 mg dose of this distillate you get nearly 1 mg of HHCP — roughly a threshold dose for that compound on its own. At 10 mg of CBN-P you're also swallowing ~2 mg of HHCP. HHCP is probably going to feel similar to what I've accidentally tried: Δ8-THC + CBN-P. The THC makes the effect of CBN-P much, much stronger: extreme alertness, the less-pleasant cousin of a heroic mindfulness session. It is the opposite of what someone taking CBN-P for sleep and emotional regulation is looking for. I refused delivery.

## What pure CBN-P looks like

For reference, a [clean COA from KCA Laboratories](coa-clean.pdf) (batch 071724.1, tested
2024-10-31) shows CBNP at 92.1% with everything else ND — no HHCP, no THC, nothing.

Here is a sample from that batch:

{{< figure src="color-sample.webp" alt="Pure CBN-P distillate showing deep reddish-brown color" >}}

Pixel spectroscopy of the liquid reveals a highly uniform deep
reddish-brown: R≈148, G≈60, B≈39 (hex `#923B27`, HSL 11°/58%/37%).

{{< figure src="color-spectrum.webp" alt="RGB channel distribution of pure CBN-P distillate sample" >}}

The three channels form tight, well-separated peaks with minimal variance.

## The grey market response

In March 2026, CBN-P started appearing across multiple vendors simultaneously:

- https://bulkcbddistributors.com/product/cbnp-distillate/
- https://oilwellcbd.com/product/cbnp-distillate/
- https://genius-genetics.com/product/cbnp-distillate/
- https://www.drganja.com/cbnp-distillate
- https://thepplsplug.com/product/cbnp-distillate/

When I asked bulkcbddistributors for a COA, they sent back a document identical to the one
MC Nutraceuticals had shared with me — matching to the least significant digit.

> KCA Labs does the testing of the product. Here is an updated COA for the batch that you
> would receive.
>
> — admin@bulkcbddistributors.com, 13 Mar 2026

The optimistic read is that some buyers genuinely don't care — maybe they want *some* kind of
psychoactive jolt and CBN-P + HHCP delivers one. More realistically, most people trying
CBN-P for the first time via this batch will conclude that CBN-P feels like extreme alertness,
write it off, and never return. A gift to future competitors and a curse on
future demand — all for a cannabinoid that genuinely deserves a market.

Manufacturing novel cannabinoid homologs is hard. But selling a failed batch as if it were a quality product is a spectacular way to ensure nobody bothers trying again.
